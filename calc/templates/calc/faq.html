What Are Macros?
  • Macronutrients, or macros, are proteins, fats, and carbohydrates. They make up the bulk of the nutrients from food and are meausured by the gram.
  • Proteins build and repair body tissue, help fight infection, and provide energy. One gram of protein is roughly 4 calories.
  • Carbohydrates are the main energy source for the body. One gram of carbohydrates is roughly 4 calories.
  • Fats provide energy and help the body absorb other nutrients. One gram of fat is roughly 9 calories.
  • Micronutrients, on the other hand, are things like vitamins, minerals, and electrolytes. They make up an essential but much smaller part of the diet and are measured by the milligram.
How Does Macro-Tracking Work?
  • Macronutrient goals are calculated for Training Days and Rest Days.
  • Training Days have a higher overall caloric and carbohydrate goal, and a lower fat goal.
  • Rest Days have a lower overall caloric and carboyhdrate goal, and a higher fat goal.
  • Both Training Days and Rest Days have a high protein goal.

What Kind of Training Should I Do?
  • On Training Days, you will do a full-body strength workout.
  • This can be done with weightlifting (squats, deadlifts, bench press, etc.), bodyweight workouts (push-ups, split-squats, inverted body-weight rows, etc.) or any high-intensity strength training.
  • Other physical activity (sports, running, etc.) can be incorporated around your training schedule, as long as it doesn't interfere with your ability to work out on Training Days.
